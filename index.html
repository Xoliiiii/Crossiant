Croissants, those deliciously flaky pastries, have a unique history. They started in Austria as "kipferl," but it was the French who made them iconic. Marie Antoinette introduced them to France in the 18th century, and French bakers transformed them into the buttery, crescent-shaped treats we adore today. From there, croissants conquered the world, evolving into a variety of flavors and shapes. Nowadays, they're not just breakfast; they're a global pastry superstar!
